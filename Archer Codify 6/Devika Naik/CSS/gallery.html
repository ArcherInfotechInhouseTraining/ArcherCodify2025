Penguins are absolutely adorable for many reasons, but one of the cutest things about them is their incredible dedication as parents. When a penguin chick hatches, the parents take turns keeping it warm and fed. The male Emperor Penguin, for instance, will hold the egg on his feet under a flap of skin called a brood pouch for two months, enduring harsh Antarctic winter conditions without eating, until the chick hatches. Once the chick is born, both parents will take turns hunting for food and keeping the chick safe. This heartwarming teamwork and devotion make penguins particularly endearing!
